Credit Card Chooser

Inspiration for Game

My friends and I came up with a fun game to decide whose credit card to use whenever we went out to eat. Instead of arguing, we turned it into a game using Google’s random number generator.

The first person would generate a random number without setting any specific range—just letting Google decide. Then, each person after would use that number as the new max for their turn, generating a new random number between 1 and the previous result.

We’d repeat this process until someone hit 0—that person had to pay the bill. It added just enough suspense and randomness to make splitting dinner both fair and entertaining.

Minor Problems

Problem 1

Manual Update

Since Google doesn't know our game rules, we had to manually update the maximum number in the range, which took some time

Problem 2

Subject to Inaccuracy

Because the process was manual, my friends would sometimes enter the wrong number—whether intentionally or not—resulting in an incorrect range

How to Play

  1. Decide who goes first and the order of sequence
  2. First person inputs a number and then every person after randomizes the range by pressing 'Submit'
  3. Repeat this process until the number hits 1. That person loses and pays using their Credit Card!
